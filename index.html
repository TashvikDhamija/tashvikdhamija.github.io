Tashvik Dhamija

MS in AII @ Carnegie Mellon University  ·  ML Intern @ AWS Annapurna Labs

Pittsburgh, PA

tdhamija@andrew.cmu.edu

I am a Master’s student in Artificial Intelligence and Innovation at Carnegie Mellon University’s School of Computer Science, where I work at the intersection of generative AI, computer vision, and efficient deep learning systems. My research spans diffusion models, causal interpretability in robot learning, and zero-shot detection methods. Currently, I am an ML Intern at Amazon AWS Annapurna Labs, enabling state-of-the-art diffusion and flow-matching model training on Trainium hardware.

Before CMU, I co-founded an AI startup at Inria Startup Studio, building real-time audio-driven avatar generation systems that were showcased at VivaTech (Paris) and Bits & Pretzels (Munich). I have published across top venues including IJCV, IEEE T-BIOM, and Applied Intelligence, with over 128 citations. I am actively exploring opportunities in ML engineering and applied AI research — feel free to reach out.
